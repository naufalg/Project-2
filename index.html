    I'd always love Studio Ghibli, they always perceived as the maestro of fantasy-animated-movie that gives people lingering-tingling-after-movie-feeling. That perspective ended after I, ignorantly watched Grave of the Fireflies expecting served by astonishing visual, fantastic soundtrack, and graceful story. Well, all of them partly are true, BUT, still give me satisfying feeling even didn't fulfilled my expectation. Why? let me explain,


    [***SPOILER ALERT***]
    (read responsibly)


    Hotaru no Haka takes place in 1945, shortly after the end of World War II bombing. A teenage boy, Seita and his sister, Setsuko, struggling to survive, scavenging food in order to survive at all cost. Meanwhile, their mother passed away caused by bombing. Setsuko, who is just a child, not yet grasped the concept of passed away. His brother desperately convincing her that their mother is doing fine. (at this rate, it makes me thinking how safe and peaceful world nowadays, so save your complaints and love each other bois). In short, everything going to be dire state and storytelled in backward style, it was so subtle I did realized it only after the third times rewatching, how cool was that?
